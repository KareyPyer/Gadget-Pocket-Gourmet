# 🍜 Mentsuyu (めんつゆ)

> *“The essence of noodles, concentrated into liquid memory.” — Dr Umami*

---

## 🧬 Identity

- **Japanese Name:** めんつゆ
- **Romanization:** Mentsuyu
- **Category:** Sauce / Soup Base
- **Origin:** Japan (modern culinary standardization of dashi-based broths)
- **Flavor Profile:** Umami-rich, salty, slightly sweet, deep dashi aroma

---

## 🧪 Description

Mentsuyu is a concentrated soup base used for Japanese noodles. It is built upon **dashi**, typically combined with soy sauce, mirin, and sugar.

It acts as a **universal noodle flavor engine** that can be diluted depending on the dish.

Dr Umami defines it as:

> “A compressed ocean of umami designed for rapid culinary deployment.”

---

## 🍜 Traditional Uses

- Soba noodles 🍜
- Udon noodles 🍥
- Cold noodle dipping sauce (tsuyu)
- Tempura dipping sauce (tentsuyu, diluted version)
- Donburi seasoning base

---

## 🧬 Core Ingredients

- Dashi (kombu + katsuobushi broth)
- Shoyu (soy sauce)
- Mirin (sweet rice wine)
- Sugar
- Sake (optional, depth enhancer)

---

## 🧪 Variants

- **Kaketsuyu:** for hot noodle soup (diluted stronger broth)
- **Tsuketsuyu:** for dipping noodles (more concentrated)
- **Homemade artisan mentsuyu:** adjusted ratios per household

## ⚗️ Dilution Protocol

Typical usage ratios:

- Hot noodles: 1 part mentsuyu → 3–5 parts water
- Cold dipping sauce: 1 part mentsuyu → 2–3 parts water
- Tempura sauce: 1 part mentsuyu → 4–6 parts water

---

## 🧊 Storage

- Refrigerate after opening
- Shake before use
- Flavor intensifies slightly over time
