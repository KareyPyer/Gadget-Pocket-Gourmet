# 🍢 Yakitori Sauce (焼き鳥のたれ)

> *“Minimal ingredients. Maximum grilled perfection.” — Dr Umami*

---

## 🧬 Identity

- **Japanese Name:** 焼き鳥のたれ
- **Romanization:** Yakitori no tare
- **Category:** Sauce / Glaze
- **Origin:** Japan (traditional street food culture)
- **Flavor Profile:** Sweet-savory, smoky, umami-rich, slightly sticky

---

## 🧪 Description

Yakitori sauce, known as **tare**, is a thick, savory-sweet glaze used for grilled chicken skewers.

Unlike teriyaki, yakitori tare is often **reduced repeatedly during cooking**, building layers of flavor over time — sometimes from a decades-old “master sauce pot.”

Dr Umami calls it:

> “A living sauce that evolves through memory and fire.”

---

## 🍜 Traditional Uses

- Chicken skewers (yakitori)
- Grilled vegetables
- Meat glazing
- Charcoal grilling enhancement
- Street food seasoning

---

## 🧬 Core Ingredients

- Shoyu (soy sauce)
- Mirin
- Sake
- Sugar
- Chicken bones or offcuts (optional, for depth)
- Ginger (optional)
