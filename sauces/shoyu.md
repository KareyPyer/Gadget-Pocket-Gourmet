# 🍶 Shoyu (醤油)

> *“The backbone of Japanese flavor engineering.” — Dr Umami*

---

## 🧬 Identity

- **Japanese Name:** 醤油
- **Romanization:** Shōyu
- **English Name:** Soy sauce
- **Category:** Sauce
- **Origin:** Japan (fermented adaptation from ancient Chinese jiang)
- **Flavor Profile:** Salty, umami-rich, slightly sweet, fermented depth

---

## 🧪 Description

Shoyu is the foundational sauce of Japanese cuisine.  
It is produced by fermenting soybeans, wheat, salt, and koji mold over several months (or years).

Dr Umami classifies it as:

> “A liquid memory of fermentation.”

---

## 🍜 Traditional Uses

- Sushi and sashimi dipping sauce
- Ramen broth seasoning
- Teriyaki glaze base
- Stir-fry seasoning
- Soup enhancement

---

## 🧬 Variants

- **Koikuchi Shoyu:** Dark, all-purpose (most common)
- **Usukuchi Shoyu:** Light-colored, saltier
- **Tamari:** Wheat-free, richer umami
- **Saishikomi Shoyu:** Double-fermented, intense flavor
