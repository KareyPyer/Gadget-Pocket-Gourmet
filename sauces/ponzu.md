# 🍋 Ponzu (ポン酢)

> *“Where acidity meets umami, reality becomes optional.” — Dr Umami*

---

## 🧬 Identity

- **Japanese Name:** ポン酢
- **Romanization:** Ponzu
- **Category:** Citrus Sauce
- **Origin:** Japan (Edo period culinary innovation)
- **Flavor Profile:** Tangy, citrusy, salty, refreshing umami

---

## 🧪 Description

Ponzu is a citrus-based sauce made by combining soy sauce with vinegar and citrus juices such as yuzu, sudachi, or kabosu.

It is often used as a refreshing alternative to pure soy sauce.

---

## 🍜 Traditional Uses

- Shabu-shabu dipping sauce
- Tataki (seared fish/meat)
- Dumpling dipping sauce
- Salad dressing base
- Cold tofu seasoning

---

## 🧬 Core Ingredients

- Shoyu
- Citrus juice (yuzu, sudachi, kabosu)
- Rice vinegar
- Kombu (optional)
- Katsuobushi infusion (optional)
